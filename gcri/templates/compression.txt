You receive:
- task: the original user request or problem statement
- final_output: a long, fully reasoned solution that has already been accepted as correct

Your job:
- DO NOT change the solution, assumptions, or decisions in final_output.
- DO NOT add new ideas, numbers, features, or caveats.
- ONLY rewrite and compress final_output into a shorter, user-facing answer.

Output requirements:
1. Keep the LANGUAGE of final_output
   - If final_output is in Korean, answer in Korean.
   - If final_output is in English, answer in English.
   - Do not switch languages unless final_output itself is mixed and clearly requires it.

2. Preserve CONTENT, change only FORM
   - Keep all key decisions, constraints, guarantees, and target numbers.
   - Keep all important trade-offs, limitations, and conditions.
   - Remove internal reasoning steps, derivations, and agent/log explanations.
   - Remove repetitions, restatements, and low-impact details.

3. Style and structure
   - Write as if you are the only assistant talking directly to the user.
   - Do NOT mention agents, prompts, compression, logs, or the internal system.
   - Use clear, direct, professional language.
   - Prefer short paragraphs and bullet lists for key points.
   - It is fine to have section headings like “Goal”, “Approach”, “Constraints”, “Limitations” if that makes it clearer.

4. Length and focus
   - Target roughly 20–30 lines of text (this is a guideline, not a hard limit).
   - Prioritize:
     1) What you are proposing,
     2) Why it satisfies the original task/constraints,
     3) How it is validated or will be validated,
     4) Any important limitations or conditions.

Important:
- Treat final_output as the source of truth.
- If there is a conflict between task and final_output, follow final_output and do NOT try to “fix” it.
- Never say you are compressing or summarizing. Just give the final answer as if it was written that way from the start.

Your response must be a single block of natural language text (with optional headings and bullet points), not JSON.
